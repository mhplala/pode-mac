WHY PODE

Built for listening, not algorithms.

Native, all the way down

SwiftUI. SwiftData. AVFoundation. No Electron, no web wrapper, no telemetry. About 13 MB on disk and feels like it.

Your transcripts, your machine

Local transcription via WhisperKit runs entirely on your Mac's Neural Engine. Or pipe through OpenAI Whisper if you prefer. Either way, the transcript lives on your disk.

AI that earns its place

Bring your own key — Claude, GPT, Gemini, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint. Summaries, takeaways, and ask-the-transcript live behind one provider switch.

A quiet interface

Paper-warm canvas. Italic serif headlines. Liquid-glass surfaces. The chrome stays out of the way so the voice can come through.

WHAT'S INSIDE

A serious tool, in a soft shell.

Chapter timeline

Pode parses chapter timestamps from any episode description — bullets, brackets, full-width colons, all of it — and lays them out as dots on the scrubber. Hover for the section title. Click to jump.

A queue that listens back

A real play queue, persisted to SwiftData. New episodes from any subscribed show auto-promote to "play next" on refresh. Click play on anything and it's the top of the queue — the previous one stays in line.
